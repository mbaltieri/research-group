Shun-ichi Amari, a mathematical neuroscientist and research advisor at 
Araya Inc., has been awarded the 40th (2025) Kyoto Prize in the Advanced 
Technology category.

This prestigious award recognizes his profound achievements: “Pioneering 
Contributions to Establishing the Theoretical Foundations of Artificial 
Intelligence and Establishing Information Geometry.”
Amari’s groundbreaking research in artificial neural networks, coupled 
with his establishment of the new field of information geometry and his 
proposal of numerous crucial theories, has profoundly influenced various 
domains.
His contributions span both theoretical advancements and practical 
applications.

The Kyoto Prize is an international award originating in Japan, presented 
to individuals who have made significant contributions to the fields of 
science and technology as well as arts and philosophy. 
Each year, a total of three individuals, one from each of these three 
fields, are selected and honored.

Operating Organization: Inamori Foundation (Public Interest Incorporated
Foundation)
